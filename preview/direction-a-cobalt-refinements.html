Cobalt refinement pass

Direction A, narrowed into three cobalt-led finals.

We’re keeping the editorial wordmark and only tuning the temperature and restraint of the cobalt system. The choice now is whether AIKB should feel neutral and crisp, slightly warmer and more human, or darker and more operator-grade.

Soft cobalt

The cleanest and most balanced option. Premium, quiet, and easy to extend across docs and product UI.

AIKB
Balanced, modern, durable
  • Accent: `#60A5FA`
  • Best for: broadest flexibility
  • Risk: slightly safer than the other two

Mineral cobalt

A touch warmer in the lettering, which makes the brand feel more authored without losing its technical edge.

AIKB
Editorial, premium, less clinical
  • Accent: `#7DD3FC`
  • Best for: a slightly more human, more designed presence
  • Risk: needs care so the warmth stays subtle

Deep signal

The most operator-grade version. Higher contrast and a sharper blue that feels closest to infrastructure tooling.

AIKB
Sharper, darker, more technical
  • Accent: `#3B82F6`
  • Best for: a stricter systems-software feel
  • Risk: can drift a little conventional if overused
Current recommendation: Mineral cobalt feels the most considered. It keeps the confidence of cobalt but adds just enough warmth to feel like a branded product rather than a default dev tool.
